Passive Scalars in Stratified Turbulence

1 — Scalar mixing layers in decaying homogeneous turbulence

Based on case III in de Bruyn Kops and Riley (2019). The flow is initially homogeneous and isotropic in power-law decay. At dimensionless time \(t^* = 1\), the density field is initialized with a uniform gradient in \(z\) and stratified turbulence develops. Three passive scalar fields are imposed: two scalar mixing layers varying in \(y\) and \(z\) respectively, and a narrow plume in \(x\).

2 — Passive scalar in stationary homogeneous sheared stratified turbulence

Based on case R1 in Portwood et al. (2022). Shear and gravity are tuned so that the flow is quasi-stationary. The active scalar has \(Pr = 7\) and the passive scalar has \(Pr = 50\), both with the same stationary mean gradient. The passive scalar starts identical to the density; finer scales develop as time advances. Top panel shows the full \(xz\) slice; lower panels show passive scalar and density in a subdomain at full resolution.
